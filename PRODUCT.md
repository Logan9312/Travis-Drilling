# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in Onoway, Alberta and the surrounding rural region who need water well work: farmers and acreage owners needing a new well drilled, and residential owners needing an existing well serviced, repaired, tested, shocked, abandoned, or a pump installed. They arrive with a concrete problem (no water, low pressure, a failing pump, a new build) and want to know quickly: does this company do my job, do they serve my area, and how do I reach them. Many are on mobile, possibly with poor rural signal, often in a hurry. The job to be done is: confirm Travis Drilling is the right local outfit and place a phone call.

## Product Purpose

A small informational website for Travis Drilling LTD, a locally owned and operated water well drilling business. It exists to clearly present who the company is, what services it offers, and the area it serves, then make it effortless to call for an estimate. Success is a visitor understanding the company within seconds and picking up the phone. It is not a lead-funnel growth machine; it is an honest digital storefront for a real local crew.

## Brand Personality

Local and personal. Family-owned, neighborly, rooted in the Onoway area and proud of it. The voice is plain, helpful, and direct: a knowledgeable tradesperson telling you straight what they do, not a salesperson. Calm confidence earned through competence, not adjectives. Three words: local, dependable, plainspoken.

## Anti-references

- **Slogans and marketing hooks.** No "empowering your water future", no taglines, no hero copy that sells a feeling. This is the hardest constraint: every line of copy must convey a real fact (a service, an area, a way to reach them), never a pitch.
- **The generic blue-and-white contractor template.** The interchangeable local-business site with stock handshake photos and a "Why Choose Us" grid of identical icon cards.
- **Corporate oil & gas energy.** Cold, faceless big-industry branding. This is a small local water-well crew, not an oilfield major.
- **Trendy startup / SaaS aesthetics.** Gradient-heavy, glassy, techy. Wrong register for a drilling business.

## Design Principles

- **Facts over hooks.** Every word earns its place by informing, not persuading. If a sentence is a slogan, cut it.
- **No unverified claims.** Never state credentials, warranties, certifications, or years-in-business that haven't been confirmed by the owner. When in doubt, omit it rather than guess. Trust comes from honest specifics, not borrowed industry boilerplate.
- **Calling is one tap away.** The phone number is always reachable; the whole site quietly funnels toward the call without nagging.
- **Show the real company.** Real local place (Onoway, Alberta), real services, real equipment imagery over decorative abstraction. Concrete beats generic.
- **Concise and scannable.** A visitor with a problem should find the answer fast. Short sections, clear service descriptions, no filler.
- **Built for rural mobile.** Fast, legible, works on a phone with a weak signal in a field.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text and the phone CTA must meet contrast minimums against their backgrounds (the hero text over imagery especially). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. Large, easily tappable phone/contact targets for users on mobile and for older or less tech-comfortable visitors.

## Scope Decisions

- **Imagery:** Real job-site photos are in place (`rig.jpg`, `rig_cropped.jpg`, `Drilling_Pit.png` in `static/img/`). More owner-supplied photos (rig setups, trucks, crew) drop in cleanly by swapping the `src`. No glossy stock — real site/equipment photos are the look.
- **About page:** Keep it short and factual — who runs it, how long, the service area, what they do. No origin-story fluff.
- **Contact:** Phone and email only. No inquiry form, no backend. The phone call is the conversion.
- **Visual direction:** Not modeled on competitor sites. Competitor research is for content gaps only (what services/info a water-well visitor expects), not aesthetic.
- **Services to showcase (confirmed real):** well drilling; well servicing & repair; pump installation & repair; water testing; well abandonment & shocking/disinfection; backhoe & trenching. (Free on-site consultation is also a real service but is currently not shown on the site.)
- **Service area:** List the specific towns covered, followed by "and nearby areas." (Final town list to be confirmed by the owner; never claim a town that isn't actually served.)
- **Brand color:** Deep Sky Blue `#00bfff` is fixed — it's the logo color (logo is black + `#00bfff`). Never change it. The supporting palette (logo black / deep ink, clean neutrals) is built around it; differentiation from generic contractor sites comes from type, spacing, restraint, and real photos, not from altering the blue.
- **No "what to expect" / process section and no FAQ.** The site showcases what Travis does; the phone call handles the how. Anything explanatory that reads as corporate gets cut.
